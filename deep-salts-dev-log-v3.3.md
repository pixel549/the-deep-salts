**Balance ruling — bounding the parry/follow-up loop (Ruleset → v8.8).** Jake flagged that Tier 1 parries had become almost his only combat strategy: one successful stopwatch check was routinely buying 2–3 consecutive unanswered player turns (the guaranteed weak-point hit, plus however long "ends its turn" was read as lasting), crowding out attrition, weapon variety, and everything else built across the last several passes. Root cause wasn't that parries were too easy to land — it's that "ends its turn" had no stated limit and stacked with the free weak-point hit into an open-ended lockout. Fix: **the payoff is now explicitly bounded to exactly one interrupted attack, one skipped enemy turn, one guaranteed weak-point hit.** The enemy is back to acting, and back to being a real threat, the turn after — keeping a chain going now requires landing another parry with the same whiff risk as the first, not one success buying indefinite safety. Tier 2/3 (bosses) were already fine and untouched — they never granted a lost turn to begin with, only Tier 1 did. The Flailer/boss ballistic-chain entries (Bible §2/§1) that grant "Tier 1 Open" on a full parried chain automatically inherit the same bound, since Tier 1's definition is centralized in Ruleset §9 — no separate edits needed there, and no inconsistency introduced. Also restated plainly in the same section: plain attacks (≤1.0 multiplier) always land with zero roll — that's the standing no-timer alternative this was always meant to sit alongside, not lose to by default. Pure rebalance, no new mechanic added.

**Follow-up refinement, same session — the Tier 1 hit stops being automatic (Ruleset → v8.9, Bible → v4.1).** One round after the turn-bounding fix above, Jake's next note: even bounded to one turn, an *automatic* weak-point hit still meant the follow-up strike carried zero risk — the only real check in the whole exchange was the parry itself. Fix: **the Tier 1 follow-up is now its own stopwatch check**, not a freebie — flattened to a flat ±0.25s tolerance regardless of the target's actual multiplier band (the loosest band in the whole Precision Strike table, §10), rather than skipped outright. Miss consequence is deliberately mild — falls back to a plain torso hit, full raw, no bonus multiplier, no further penalty — since the target's already down and defenseless; a miss costs the bonus, not a beating. Tier 2/3 untouched (they were never automatic to begin with — Tier 2 already widened one tier from actual, Tier 3 already stayed full). All six Bible references to the old "Tier 1 Open ... no second stopwatch" wording (Bathhouse Flailer, Reel-Torn Usher, Shuttle-Armed Weaver, Ream-Wrapped Binder, Foundry Marshal, Bellfounder) updated to match — they inherit Tier 1's definition from Ruleset §9, so the wording needed fixing in place rather than relying on the cross-reference alone. Net effect across both rulings this session: parrying is still the strongest single read in the game, but every step of the payoff — the parry, and now the follow-up — carries its own real (if generous) chance to miss, rather than one check buying a chain of guaranteed value.
