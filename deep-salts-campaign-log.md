First met partially mutated behind an Insight-gated door (session zero/one); met again this session in a completely different layout of the same recipe — "whatever I am now, I'm not one of the things that gets reshuffled with the room." No-names rule when speaking with him, unexplained. Talkative, trades honest question for honest question, currently the best lead on how the world works. **Open request:** bring him a taste of genuinely clean source-water if one is found (explicitly optional, his words). **Open question:** whether he persists in *other* recipes too — he doesn't know himself; brewing the Choir Deep will test it.

- **The Choir Deep.** Recipe identified, unbrewed, sitting in Lloyd's room two sessions running. Doubles as the persistence experiment for the old man.
- **The Beckoner (carried item).** Instance quest *completed* — encountered, recovered, identified in-run, extracted as a persistent item. Its ongoing tradeoff (1 Influence stack on Lloyd per use, uncleared while carried) is live whenever he first uses it. Its warning lore ("if it asks for your name, don't—") travels with it.
- **The Charm (carried item).** Now usable for the first time — Insight sits exactly at its 6+ gate. Untested.
- **The Wooden Token (carried item).** Keepsake of "R.," a person from a now-expired instance. No mechanical effect; kept for whatever narrative weight it accrues. The R. story itself is gone with its layout — the token is all that escaped.

- Combat resolves fast and concrete — numbers first, minimal narrative padding on routine hits. Description gets more room for kills, called shots, and narrative beats (NPC dialogue, environment, discoveries).
- Player prefers real stopwatch checks (their own phone/count) over blind in-chat timing widgets where possible — the blind widget attempt in session zero was a genuine miss on DM's part, not a mechanic to repeat.
- Session-zero rulings (visceral charges removed, Influence uncapped, enemy limb stagger duration, etc.) are now written directly into ruleset v7.1 and design bible v2.6 — a fresh instance reading those documents will already have them; no need to re-explain or re-litigate.
- Jake (the player) is also this game's designer — expect him to spot rule inconsistencies mid-play and to want them resolved with concrete numbers on the spot, not deferred. Flag gaps rather than silently patching them, same as the design-process norms already established.
- Session 2 introduced live DM-improvised mechanics mid-fight (a "Dragged" status, Heavy-attacks-break-grapples ruling) that got defined properly on request rather than left vague — same pattern going forward: improvise when needed, then nail down the number the moment it's asked for.
