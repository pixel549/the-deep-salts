## Established tone / running notes for continuity

- Combat resolves fast and concrete — numbers first, minimal narrative padding on routine hits. Description gets more room for kills, called shots, and narrative beats (NPC dialogue, environment, discoveries).
- Player prefers real stopwatch checks (their own phone/count) over blind in-chat timing widgets where possible.
- Jake (the player) is also this game's designer — expect him to spot rule inconsistencies mid-play and want them resolved with concrete numbers on the spot, not deferred. Flag gaps rather than silently patch them.
- Improvise when needed, then nail the number down the moment it's asked for — session 3 examples: Powder Charge detonation timing, the Insight tier window-bonus reading, ambush called shots stacking with visceral, the whole Abandon-All/Retreat split.
- **Turn order changed this session:** no more d20 roll-off. Player always acts first, every round; enemies act as a block after. Written into ruleset §17. Direct consequence: enemy tells and their payoff attacks can now resolve within a single enemy turn rather than needing an artificial multi-round split.
- Jake caught several live DM errors this session (double-dipping vs. not-stacking multipliers on the ambush kill, offering a "blind parry" that isn't a real mechanic, forgetting Lloyd's action was already spent, under-running a Penitent's approach for several rounds) — all corrected in play when flagged, all now reflected in the docs. Worth continuing to double-check stacking/ordering of bonuses and whose action-economy pool is actually being spent before narrating a turn's options.
- Ad-hoc SVG battle maps (via create_file, simple top-down sketches with a legend) worked well for keeping combat positioning clear — worth reaching for again when a fight has more than two or three bodies on the field.
