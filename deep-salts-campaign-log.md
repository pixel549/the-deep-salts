## Open narrative threads (don't forget these)

- **The old man at the lantern.** Found kneeling in the first biome's flooded colonnade, partially mutated (wrong hand, too many joints), stuck behind an Insight-gated door he can no longer pass. Lloyd tried carrying him through the threshold physically — per the session's ruling, the door only judges the person crossing, not what they carry, so the old man simply didn't come through. No confirmation either way whether this helped, hurt, or did nothing. He's presumably still exactly where Lloyd left him unless a future session revisits that location.
- **The reception clerk's "brother" comments.** The clerk has twice referenced Lloyd's "brother" currently taking the cure, and later said "your brother asked about ways too, near the end" — deliberately unconfirmed whether this is a real person, a mistake, manipulation, or something the location does to everyone who walks in. Lloyd has flatly denied having a brother both times. Unresolved on purpose — a genuine hook, not a loose end to tidy up.
- **The Choir Deep.** Recipe identified, unbrewed. Next biome whenever Lloyd chooses to walk in.
- **The Charm.** Carried, currently unusable — needs Insight Tier 3 (Insight 6+); Lloyd's at Insight 4 (Tier 2). Will become active once he levels Insight further.

## Established tone / running notes for continuity

- Combat resolves fast and concrete — numbers first, minimal narrative padding on routine hits. Description gets more room for kills, called shots, and narrative beats (NPC dialogue, environment, discoveries).
- Player prefers real stopwatch checks (their own phone/count) over blind in-chat timing widgets where possible — the blind widget attempt in session zero was a genuine miss on DM's part, not a mechanic to repeat.
- Session-zero rulings (visceral charges removed, Influence uncapped, enemy limb stagger duration, etc.) are now written directly into ruleset v7.1 and design bible v2.6 — a fresh instance reading those documents will already have them; no need to re-explain or re-litigate.
- Jake (the player) is also this game's designer — expect him to spot rule inconsistencies mid-play and to want them resolved with concrete numbers on the spot, not deferred. Flag gaps rather than silently patching them, same as the design-process norms already established.
