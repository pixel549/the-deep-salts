## Where things stand

**Prettyboy Lloyd** just completed his second real run and is back at the hub (Stillwell Hydro), Vault banked, HP and capsules restored, Insight risen to 6 (Tier 3 — the Charm is now usable for the first time). He entered the same salt recipe as his first run (rerolled layout), fought past a rooted, leashed floor-creature in a flooded gallery without killing it, looted a chapel room (a sentient-seeming idol later identified as "The Beckoner," a keepsake token, a burned journal), met a recurring old man NPC who appears to persist across rerolls of this recipe specifically, and found this run's anchor before backtracking to explore further and banking clean. The Choir Deep recipe is still unbrewed, still sitting in his room. Next session should pick up at the hub — shopping/prepping, trying the now-usable Charm, deciding on the Beckoner's Influence-cost tradeoff, or finally brewing the Choir Deep to descend somewhere new.

## Open narrative threads (don't forget these)

- **The old man at the lantern / by the fire.** First met partially mutated behind an Insight-gated door in the flooded colonnade (session zero/one). Met again this session, in a completely different layout of the *same recipe*, past a rubble crawl, living by a fire, seemingly unaffected by rerolls — "whatever I am now, I'm not one of the things that gets reshuffled with the room." Claims to have run this game's version of salt-recipe runs "more times than I can count," until one run he touched the clean thing and "didn't end up back at any Hydro." No names allowed in conversation with him — his rule, unexplained. He asked Lloyd, unprompted, for a taste of genuinely clean source-water if he finds one. Confirmed talkative, not hostile, currently the best lead on how this world actually works.
- **"R."** — a recurring initial: the burned journal's author, and the name scratched into the wall of a cell with signs of long confinement ("R. WAS HERE"), where Lloyd found a wooden keepsake token. Journal implies R. stopped existing partway through writing it, possibly connected to the Beckoner idol's warning ("if it asks for your name, don't—"). Not yet connected to the old man or anyone else. Fully open.
- **The reception clerk's "brother" comments.** Now referenced three times total (twice in session zero/one, confirmed still live this session — Lloyd told the old man "she's mentioned a brother twice now"). Lloyd has not confirmed or denied having a brother to the old man, deliberately avoiding a direct answer both to the clerk previously and now. Still an open, deliberate hook.
- **The dragging floor-creature.** Scouted, fought (twice grabbed Lloyd, twice broken via Heavy + Powder Charge), never killed — Lloyd disengaged through a doorway once clear of its reach. Per the old man, it's rooted, non-aggressive unless provoked, has been in the flood gallery for years chasing something that "isn't native" and "never found its way home," and per the old man's own words is now audibly sulking after being "just punched" rather than tricked or bribed like everything before it. HP currently sits at 468/original pool if the same instance persists into a future reroll of this recipe — unconfirmed whether it does.
- **The Beckoner (idol).** Identified via the old man, not Maud or an Insight roll. Forces an Influence stack onto a target in line of sight; costs Lloyd 1 Influence stack per use, sourced from the idol itself, which does NOT clear via the normal source-elimination rule while he's still carrying it. A real risk/reward item — unused so far.
- **The Choir Deep.** Recipe identified, still unbrewed, still in his room. Two full sessions now without touching it.
- **The Charm.** Was unusable at Insight 4; now sits exactly at the Insight 6 threshold and is live for the first time. Untested.
- **The old man's request.** Wants a taste of genuinely clean source-water, if Lloyd finds a source that qualifies. Low-stakes, easy to forget, explicitly optional per the old man himself.

## Established tone / running notes for continuity

- Combat resolves fast and concrete — numbers first, minimal narrative padding on routine hits. Description gets more room for kills, called shots, and narrative beats (NPC dialogue, environment, discoveries).
- Player prefers real stopwatch checks (their own phone/count) over blind in-chat timing widgets where possible — the blind widget attempt in session zero was a genuine miss on DM's part, not a mechanic to repeat.
- Session-zero rulings (visceral charges removed, Influence uncapped, enemy limb stagger duration, etc.) are now written directly into ruleset v7.1 and design bible v2.6 — a fresh instance reading those documents will already have them; no need to re-explain or re-litigate.
- Jake (the player) is also this game's designer — expect him to spot rule inconsistencies mid-play and to want them resolved with concrete numbers on the spot, not deferred. Flag gaps rather than silently patching them, same as the design-process norms already established.
- Session 2 introduced live DM-improvised mechanics mid-fight (a "Dragged" status, Heavy-attacks-break-grapples ruling) that got defined properly on request rather than left vague — same pattern going forward: improvise when needed, then nail down the number the moment it's asked for.
- Session 2 caught two DM math errors (stale HP after a hub heal; a missed Rally recovery) — both corrected in play when Jake flagged them. Worth double-checking HP/Rally math proactively rather than waiting to be caught.
