## Where things stand

**Prettyboy Lloyd** is back at the hub (Stillwell Hydro) after his fourth run into the flooded-gallery recipe — the first time that recipe delivered a genuinely different layout instead of a reshuffled colonnade/pews. He woke in an undercroft beneath the areas he already knew, waded through water that turned out — at his current Insight — to be old blood rather than flood-rot, and found a crypt niche deliberately dressed different from the rest: a woman buried whole rather than fed into the rite the rest of this place was built around. A dry ritual chamber past the crypt carried the full explanation via carved relief — this recipe's whole biome sits on a bloodletting sacrament — plus one figure scraped out of the record entirely except for a single raised arm: someone who refused the rite and got erased for it. An Insight-gated door (crossed clean at Tier 4) led to a small hidden room with one word carved into the wall — **Mirel** — and floor-wear suggesting repeat visits by someone who kept coming back to sit with it.

Lloyd fought and killed a Chapel Penitent along the way — first real combat use of **the Salvage Launcher**, the engineer's ranged commission, fielded for the first time this session after three sessions of chasing his lost Metal Knuckles or farming weaponless. Clean fight: two ranged shots, a parry-floor punch combo, zero HP lost, one shallow Blood Loss stack from an earlier blind grope in the dark (cleared on hub return).

Banked the run clean via a new anchor (a font room with real clean water, distinct from everywhere else in this recipe): 14 Salts, a found Tonic, a corroded ring pulled off the dressed body, and a wax-sealed vessel from the same niche — which, once the hidden room's script became legible, turned out almost certainly to hold that same woman's blood, kept out of the rite deliberately. Back at the hub, **sold the vessel to Maud for 20 Salts** — she didn't ask whose it was, but the sale visibly meant something to her beyond commerce; her three long-standing salvage requests (sealed items, source-water, packing cloth) read very differently now, like a personal list rather than shop restocking. Vault crossed 50 as a result — first attribute point now affordable, not yet spent.

Then showed her the ring. She recognized the scratched symbol on sight, confirmed it isn't dangerous, and shut the conversation down hard when pushed on why she didn't want it near her — **"I've buried someone who wore that mark. Once."** Lloyd apologized for pushing a personal nerve, offered Mirel's name as a peace offering; Maud declined to confirm or deny the connection tonight, asked him to raise it again another time, and visibly thawed once the apology landed. Genuinely new, real thread — not resolved, not stonewalled either.

## Established tone / running notes for continuity

- Combat resolves fast and concrete — numbers first, minimal narrative padding on routine hits. Description gets more room for kills, called shots, and narrative beats (NPC dialogue, environment, discoveries).
- Player prefers real stopwatch checks (their own phone/count) over blind in-chat timing widgets where possible.
- Jake (the player) is also this game's designer — expect him to spot rule inconsistencies mid-play and want them resolved with concrete numbers on the spot, not deferred. Flag gaps rather than silently patch them.
- **DM self-correction, mid-session (session 5):** let a parry-floor + whiff-recovery window run for a third consecutive free called shot before catching that "long recovery" had already been generously extended once and shouldn't stretch further. Corrected forward (not retroactively) by ruling the third hit a self-initiated called shot instead. Separately, defaulted to the Launcher on a called shot right after Jake had explicitly said he was saving ammo — caught immediately, redone with Fists, no lasting inconsistency. Both logged as the kind of live-play correction this campaign runs on rather than something to paper over.
- **New weapon design discipline, learned the hard way this session:** a freshly-commissioned weapon (the Salvage Launcher) was first drafted as Metal Knuckles with a lower base number and a renamed passive — Jake caught the reskin immediately. Rebuilt around what the weapon should actually trade on (range, guaranteed payload, finite ammo) rather than copying an existing entry's shape. Worth remembering for any future weapon: identity first, numbers second.
- Sub-locale variation (Ruleset §18, v7.2) got its first real payoff this session — the flooded-gallery recipe finally showed something other than the colonnade/pews loop from sessions 1, 2, and 4.
- Instance flavour tagging also got its first real use — this reroll ran lore/mystery-primary with one contained fight, not combat-primary the way the last three visits to this recipe did.
