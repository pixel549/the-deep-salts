## Established tone / running notes for continuity

- Combat resolves fast and concrete — numbers first, minimal narrative padding on routine hits. Description gets more room for kills, called shots, and narrative beats (NPC dialogue, environment, discoveries).
- Player prefers real stopwatch checks (their own phone/count) over blind in-chat timing widgets where possible.
- Jake (the player) is also this game's designer — expect him to spot rule inconsistencies mid-play and want them resolved with concrete numbers on the spot, not deferred. Flag gaps rather than silently patch them.
- **Low-stakes/zero-threat combat gets batched, not resolved swing-by-swing** — session 4's Waterlogged Guest farm ran on batched dice (player rolling 3–6 saves at once) once the pattern was established, with the DM doing the arithmetic across the batch in one pass. Good template for future trivial fights: nail the first exchange in full detail, then batch the rest once the shape of the fight is clear.
- **DM self-correction, mid-session:** briefly awarded a first-sighting Insight bump for the Waterlogged Guest before Jake caught that it wasn't actually a new archetype — corrected in place, and turned into the new standing rule (a complete Monster Manual entry means it's already been encountered, full stop). A second, separate error later in the session (forgetting a Camphor use when reporting end-of-run Insanity) was also caught and corrected before the docs were finalized. Worth continuing to double check status-track math specifically — that's where both slips happened this session, not combat math.
- Improvise when needed, then nail the number down the moment it's asked for — session 4 example: bare-handed damage numbers, invented live the moment Lloyd went into a fight weaponless, then logged properly afterward.
