**Session 7 documentation sweep — contradictions found and resolved:**
· **Boss spawn rate (Ruleset §18).** Ruleset previously said a killed boss could still generate fresh instances; campaign log said the Singer would never respawn. **Ruling:** guaranteed spawn every reroll while undefeated; after the first kill a landmark settles and it drops to a rare spawn, ~1 reroll in 4 (same rarity band as a found in-run NPC). Respawns are always fresh and full-HP.
· **Two new archetypes (Ruleset §16), replacing mislabels.** Salt-Eaten Custodian was tagged Shambler and Drowned Bellkeeper was tagged Chanter — both archetypes are defined as unparryable, yet both monsters have parryable telegraphed attacks that were actually parried in session 7. Rather than relabel to existing archetypes, two new ones were added: **DRUDGE** (elite, ~600–900 HP, slow and relentless, never bursts, one always-parryable telegraphed heavy, Tier 1–2 — the readable counterpoint to Shambler) and **TOLLER** (mook, ~250–400 HP, track pressure via a **destructible implement**, Tier 1 — destroy the tool and it has no attack left, the counterpoint to Chanter).
· **Marble Effigy → Marble Attendant (Bible §1, Monster 7).** Was tagged Effigy, which Ruleset §16 reserves as a boss-tier duellist/mirror archetype (7m+ movement, parries and viscerals the player). The entry had none of that — 2m move, one plain heavy. Reclassified as a Drudge elite at Tier 2 and renamed to free the Effigy name for an actual mirror boss later. Its own flavour text already called it a carved attendant statue.
· **Bathhouse Flailer ballistic chain (Bible §1, Monster 6).** Flailer's defining trait per §16 is "multiple windows in sequence, Tier 3, hardest non-boss to open" — the entry had two ordinary single-window attacks. **Frenzied Whirl is now a real 3-beat chain:** windows at 0.80s / 1.30s / 1.80s, each ±0.20s base, 30 raw per unparried beat. All three parried = Tier 1 Open; miss any beat and the rest of the chain lands automatically. Towel Lash demoted to unparryable chip so the chain is the only opening.
· **The Singer's sever threshold (Bible §1, Monster 8).** Limb table said ×2.0 / threshold 400 (= 200 raw by the standard formula); the Boss Gimmick prose said "400 raw-equivalent." **Limb table wins** — 200 raw in one hit. Prose corrected. Deliberately above current weapon reach; sever was never the required path.
· **Tier column corrected across all monster entries (Bible §1).** Every attack table had the Tier column filled with the attack's weight (Light/Heavy — already in the Type column) instead of the post-parry weak-point tier from Ruleset §9. All eight entries now carry real tiers (1/2/3/N-A).
· **Implements and objects (Ruleset §10).** Clarified: a carried bell, shield, or eye is **not a limb** — it's an extension of the enemy, targeted the way a weapon is. It **deals full HP damage** like any other hit (raw × multiplier) and uses **Sever only**, no partial stagger state. Previously the "Sever only" phrasing was read as implying no HP damage, which was wrong.
· **Duplication cuts (Bible).** Removed content that duplicated the ruleset rather than extending it, per the project's own rule of use: the enemy-variety discipline paragraph (Ruleset §18), the damage formula and sever-maths block (Ruleset §4/§10), the limb constants in the blank monster template, and the Blood Loss / Rupture status entries (Ruleset §5). Also cut the superseded strikethrough Beckoner text — its history lives here instead.
· **Stale-reference fixes.** The Beckoner and the Charm both still read "currently lost with the Singer" despite being recovered in session 6. Ruleset and bible version headers were still stamped 8.0 / 3.0. Campaign log described session 6's attribute point as Lloyd's "first" — it was his third (session zero spent two: Skill 16→17, Resolve 2→3). Every Vault figure across all seven sessions reconciles: 9 → 15 → 15 → 18 → 52 → 94 → 131.
